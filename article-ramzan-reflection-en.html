When Half of Ramadan Passes, the Heart Awakens... Is Life Passing the Same Way?

March 5, 2026 | Reading Time: 12 min

When the sun of Ramadan reaches its fifteenth station, a sudden strange feeling surrounds the heart. Only fifteen days ago, we were busy preparing to welcome this holy month, and now it is already racing toward the countdown. Half of Ramadan has passed! This sentence does not just inform us of the passage of time; it is a call for **accountability** (Muhasabah).

But think for a moment! Is our life not also moving silently toward its destination in the same way? Just as the days melt away in the Suhoor and Iftar of Ramadan, the pearls of our life's necklace are falling one by one. If our heart awakens at the passing of half of Ramadan, do we not realize at the threshold of old age OR when losing a loved one that perhaps more than half of our life has also passed in silence?

1. Self-Accountability: What Have I Gained and What Have I Lost?

In Islamic teachings, **Muhasabah** (self-accountability) is considered a great attribute of a believer. Hazrat Umar Farooq (R.A) said: *"Hold yourself accountable before you are held accountable."*

After half of Ramadan has passed, we must look within ourselves:

  • Have I found that "Taqwa" (God-consciousness) that Allah promised in this month?
  • Has my Quranic recitation produced an effect that can change my character?
  • What have I gained that will stay with me in the darkness of the grave?
  • And what have I lost through negligence and laziness that will never return?

2. The Reality of Life and the Sands of Time

Time is like sand slipping through fingers; the more you try to hold it tightly, the faster it falls. The passage of half of Ramadan is a sign that instead of crying over **"lost time,"** we should make the **"remaining time"** valuable.

In the Holy Quran, Allah Almighty says: *"And that there is not for man except that [good] for which he strives"* (Surah An-Najm). If we have wasted the early years of our life, not learned the Word of Allah, or been negligent in prayers, has the time not come for us to return to our Creator?

Stop for a Moment and Think!

Just as the last ten days of Ramadan are the essence of the whole month, if the last part of life is enlightened by repentance and Quranic teachings, the whole life can be saved. Would you not want your journey of learning the Quran to start today?

3. Connection with the Quran: The Only Light in a Dark Life

After traveling half the distance of life, when a traveler starts to feel tired, they need light. That light is **Al-Quran**. If until today you have only read it without understanding, or if your children are still unaware of the beautiful teachings of the Quran, this is the biggest loss that will never return.

4. What Have I Gained That Will Stay With Me?

Worldly degrees, wealth, bank balances—all these will be left here. What will go with us? Only the knowledge you learned and acted upon. The Prophet ﷺ said: *"The best among you is the one who learns the Quran and teaches it."*

If you give your children Islamic upbringing and make them Hufaz of the Quran, this is the investment that will continue to increase your good deeds even after death. This is the "gain" that will always be with you.

5. Action Plan for the Remaining Days of Ramadan

If you have wasted half of Ramadan, do not despair. Allah's door is always open. Do the following in the remaining days:

  • Sincere Repentance (Tawbah): Ask forgiveness for past sins with a sincere heart.
  • Recitation and Understanding: Start reading the Quran at least once with translation.
  • Charity (Sadaqah): Take out a portion for the poor from your life.
  • Seeking Knowledge: Connect with a good academy like Saeed Quran Academy to increase your knowledge.

Conclusion: The Present Moment is Your Whole Life

Spent days will not return, and there is no guarantee for the coming days. What exists is only "Now." Half of Ramadan gives us the message to wake up from the bed of negligence and run toward Allah's mercy. Life is passing silently; before the sun sets, light the lamps of your hereafter.
